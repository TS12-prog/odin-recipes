Cookies

Description

A cookie is a baked or cooked snack or dessert that is typically small, flat and sweet. It usually contains flour, sugar, egg, and some type of oil, fat, or butter. It may include other ingredients such as raisins, oats, chocolate chips, nuts, etc.

Biscuit or cookie variants include sandwich biscuits, such as custard creams, Jammie Dodgers, Bourbons and Oreos, with marshmallow or jam filling and sometimes dipped in chocolate or another sweet coating. Cookies are often served with beverages such as milk, coffee or tea and sometimes "dunked", an approach which releases more flavour from confections by dissolving the sugars, while also softening their texture.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup white sugar
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • ½ cup butter, softened
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup chopped almonds
  • 3 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 2 teaspoons rum
  • ½ teaspoon whiskey

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C). Lightly grease cookie sheets.
  2. Combine white and brown sugar, butter and eggs. Stir in flour, baking soda and salt. The dough will be stiff.
  3. Stir in nuts, chocolate chips, rum and whiskey. Drop dough by tablespoonful onto cookie sheet. Bake 8 to 10 minutes.
